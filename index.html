About the Project

This project aims to empower and support more women and minorities in technology by connecting disadvantaged girls in India that are learning how to code with mentors who can guide them and provide resources to help them build applications that improve their community.

We are partnering with an incredible organisation already working with young women in the largest slum in Asia, the Dharavi Slum of Mumbai. Dharavi Diary: Slum and Rural Innovation Project is a storytelling, digital literacy and mobile application entrepreneurship initiative for slum community to build leadership and tech skills in women and children to become Change Makers of their community. It has created a tech design lab where women and children from slum and rural community meet to learn, unlearn and create using STEAM education, design thinking methodology and mobile application technology to solve their community problems. This will lead them realize their full potential. They will start participating in decision making of their families and communities. They will have better job opportunities, independence, self worth and self esteem.
